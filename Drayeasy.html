📊Business Context
Industry Overview

The freight forwarding industry, vital for global trade, faces challenges in efficiently managing and tracking container shipments. Drainage services for last-mile deliveries are hindered by outdated practices and inefficiencies. Freight forwarders often struggle to find reliable truckers offering good rates for these critical final stretches. Consequently, this sector urgently needs technological advancements to enable more effective and real-time logistics management.


Business goal


Product mission: To help cargo owners find the best drayage price, connect carriers, and make drayage easier.
DrayEasy streamlines "last mile" transport for North American cargo owners, offering instant quotes, container movement management, drayage services, and on-demand GPS tracking. This innovation has provided a convenient rate engine for quick pricing on most North American routes and a database of over 3000 routes, setting new industry standards.

Current users prefer email over the platform, as it takes up to 20 minutes to find container details. New users wait up to 2 days in the sign-up process.

Define & Research

🧐Issues from User Perspective

To understand the inefficiencies of the current platform, we conducted several rounds of user interviews with industry experts. These operators promptly pointed out current problems, expressed their needs, and suggested where we could enhance the design to meet their requirements.


💣Problem1: Lack of Interactivity
The current design consists of 4 sections: industry news, a map, a list of containers, and exceptions that require actions. However, the information in those four sections is not linked together interactively. Ideally, selecting a container should display its location on the map and highlight relevant news impacting it.

Consequence:
The dashboard is so noisy to the operators that they rather rely on emails instead. Currently, the only useful feature is the container list, but even this is too limited in scope for their needs.

💣Problem2: Counterintuitive Information Hierarchy
Currently, containers are nested under their corresponding orders, requiring knowledge of the specific order to locate a container. However, for operators, details like the status of an order or its associated containers are often extraneous. This indirect approach through orders forces operators to sift through multiple orders to find a particular container.

Consequence:
This structure makes it time-consuming for operators to access information on individual containers, driving them to resort to searching through their email inboxes instead for quicker retrieval.

💣Problem3: Noisy Notifications
The notification center quickly gets flooded with updates for thousands of containers. Without proper classification, operators often ignore new notifications. Additionally, notifications are purely informative with no interactive functionality within the center.

Consequence:
Operators, overwhelmed by the excessive messages in the notification center, tend to ignore it, preferring to use emails instead. Emails allow them to self-categorize and respond directly to issues, enhancing efficiency and engagement.

🚩Challenges from Product Perspective

The sign-up process takes up to 2 days
In addition to user testing, our examination of the current workflow revealed several design flaws, particularly in the sign-up process. Currently, users must complete a Google form, after which sales representatives contact, verify, and set up their accounts. This process, taking up to 2 days, starkly contrasts with Easy Drayage's promise of instant quotations. Not only does this slow down the sign-up rate, but it also creates a bottleneck in scalability and reduces commercial viability.

 

 

Competitive Analysis

Opaque Pricing Among Competitors
We discovered that none of the competitors provide instant quotations, typically requiring up to a month to gather such information. This is corroborated by our web analytics data, indicating that while many users obtain quotations from the platform, they often do not proceed to book or manage containers through it. Additionally, the order page designs of three competitors are noteworthy and could offer valuable insights for improvement.

🎨Ideate
User Flow

Introducing trial accounts for faster sign-up
Given the industry standard of verifying users, their businesses, and business licenses, fully automating the sign-up process is currently not feasible. To counter this, we suggest implementing trial accounts. These accounts, valid for 30 days without immediate verification, would permit up to 5 quotations, effectively separating the sign-up into two phases. This approach enables users to explore the platform's features without delay, while the verification process unfolds in the background.


Layout Exploration

Refining Dashboard Layouts with Expert Insights
In response to expert feedback, we explored various dashboard layout designs. We recognized the potential benefits of a map view for enhancing usability, provided it was made interactive. For instance, clicking on a port in the map could display all containers there, along with their current status and relevant industry news. Additionally, user interviews revealed a preference for the list view, with feedback highlighting the current limited size. Therefore, we decided to integrate a more expansive list view alongside the interactive map, catering to user familiarity and preferences.

📝Reflection
Human-Centric Product Strategy


Our initial approach of displaying all available information on the dashboard led to an overload, slowing users down as they sifted through the excess to find what they needed. This experience taught me the importance of understanding and prioritizing the information essential for users to complete their current tasks, displaying only the most relevant details prominently.

Next step:

Our forthcoming step is to equip users with comprehensive analytics and reports, enhancing their ability to monitor and comprehend their workflows, challenges, and requirements. This will be integrated into a new sub-page, decluttering the current container tracking dashboard. By migrating existing statistics to this dedicated space, we aim to streamline the user interface, providing more room for the map and table, and thus facilitating a more focused and efficient user experience.
