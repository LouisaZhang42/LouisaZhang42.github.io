Project 1 (Q2)

📈(1) Strategic Context

In 2023 Q2, my team was heavily developing the Draw AI feature (using the Stable Diffusion interface).
The AI creation community provided inspiration, templates, prompt demonstrations, and style model training examples for this feature. However, as the feature expanded and became more complex, user engagement declined, and the supply of quality content in the community couldn't meet the demand. Consequently, the retention rate of users decreased.

 

 

🚩(2) Project Objectives
Project Positioning

Enhance the activity level of C-end users in the AI community, increase the supply of high-quality content, ensure the balance of supply and demand in the content ecosystem, and iterate on the high-quality content community for both B-end and C-end users.

 

 

🎯(3) Actions

After conducting an in-depth study of effective motivational strategies, I led the project's progression, delivering the Product Requirement Document (PRD) and strategy tables.

 

 

💾(4) Deliver results

Assessing long-term impact

By fostering AI creators and encouraging the production of more content, we facilitate user interaction, establish a thriving community, and generate abundant user-generated content. Therefore, we leverage those C-end users to boost the growth and payment rate of B-end users.

· For Customers: (1) Guides users for quick adaptation to the community and templates, reducing the time to discover value and leading to enlightening 'Aha' moments. (2) Provides existing users with a broader range of usage methods.

Project 1 (Q2)

📈(1) Strategic Context

After Q2, we accumulated many new active users. Then, my team's goal for Q3 has shifted towards converting more users into paying customers for AI functionality. 📋Reflection
Human-Centric Product Strategy

Previously, my problem-solving approach was often confined to dissecting the issue itself, leaning towards tangible solutions. owever, when I developed user growth features, I realized that many successful companies often rely on human-centric solutions. This involves viewing people not just as users or customers, but as integral resources for problem-solving. Therefore, in future product strategies, my focus will extend beyond the product itself to encompass the whole product ecosystem and the human ecosystem around it.

Cross-functional Team Collaboration

Team communication requires a user research mindset:
When I noticed that the design team's deliveries were often later than expected, I observed their workflow and discussed it with them. Then, I identified the root cause: the design lead's overly stringent reviews, sometimes even requiring multiple revisions for details like the radius of corners. Therefore, I expressed the product team's need for rapid iteration—quickly launching solutions, assessing data, and then refining design details. This approach successfully aligned the design team's delivery times with expectations.

Importance of understanding technical aspects:
The selection of features and labels, construction of sub-modules, and the establishment and testing of model standards all require product managers to present an understanding of business scenarios and user needs. A deep understanding of technical principles can significantly improve collaboration efficiency with engineers.
